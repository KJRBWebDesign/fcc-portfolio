I wasn't one of those kids who excelled in school. Very, very far from it. But I'd always had a thirst for learning. In my early years, I would soak up the knowledge my adult counterparts would speak when they conversated with one another. Yes, I know. It seems kind of creepy in retrospect(especially for my family). But even then I had an unquenchable thirst for knowledge, I just didn't know where to look. So I looked everywhere. I narrowed my search down at around thirteen to one thing: computers. I love em'. Everything about them is mystical and magic, but when you break things down into smaller problems it becomes quite simple. Completing my first development project was like giving a child their first toy. The only difference being my toy was actually quite awful. But I loved it. I continued to make more toys until eventually, I made good toys, and here we are today.
